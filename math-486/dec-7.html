Notice that the feasibility condition is a single condition; the stability condition is separate conditions.

The feasibility condition indicates that the grand coalition must have the resources to "hand out" to the players. The sum of everybody's winnings is all they have; they can't make money out of thin air (though they can throw away money).

The stability condition ensures that the players in collectively get at least ; otherwise, they would never agree to the binding contract specifying payoff , since they could instead just break off and improve as a coalition.

The core is by far the most used concept in game theory; it's natural in that these two conditions are "obvious".

You can also rewrite conditions 1 and 2 as the following:

  • - efficiency, (3)
  • for all where - (4)
  • for - individual rationality, (4')

Efficiency is implied by (1, feasibility) and (2, stability), while (4) and (4') are implied by (2). Condition (4') essentially indicates "stability for one-player coalitions," or individual rationality. This condition is stated separately sometimes, since such 1-member coalitions will never have problems forming.
