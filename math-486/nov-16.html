It is worth noting that the "optimal threat" is "optimal" in that it represents the minimax/optimal solution to , which (approximately) expresses the "rivalry" between the players. Each element of represents the difference .

In general, both players want to maximize their share of a given combined payoff - as a result, player 1 wants to maximize , while player 2 wants to minimize . This is because larger differences of give player 1 more "bargaining power" since they're contributing more to the cooperative payoff, and therefore are entitled to a bigger share of the combined pot; smaller (or even negative) differences give player 2 more "bargaining power" in a similar manner.

In this sense, we can treat as a two-person zero-sum game, for which the solution to this game represents one position (of possibly many positions) for which if negotiations break down (which they won't), both players end up equally disadvantaged. In the payoff plane , the players will have a common liking for the "northeast" direction but a common dislike for the "southwest" direction. The minimax solution in that produces the optimal threat, and any other potential threat points, is geometrically guaranteed to lie southwest of the TU solution.

The NTU case
...

Now, consider a case in which the TU assumption does not hold; in particular, the case in which there is no TU (transferable utility) resource with which the players can bargain. For example, the payoffs in our TU games where in terms of money, but perhaps instead we might have payoffs in terms of "months in jail" or "units of satisfaction". Additionally, they might be in completely different units - perhaps one gets paid in "pounds of oranges" and another in gold. In any case, these payoffs aare not transferrable, and they may be literally different in value.

So how does this change the feasible region - that is, the set of (expected) payoffs that the players can enact? If we don't have the transferrable resource, is there any guarantee that specific values in the payoff plane are possible? Payoffs - including - might not be feasible, much less points above the line.

So what is feasible? It turns out that since the players can still coordinate the strategies, they can still generate expected payoffs along the line segment between all four payoffs in our matrix game (as an example), even if the resource isn't transferrable.
